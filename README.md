[![Focus](https://img.shields.io/badge/Focus-Behavioral_Systems-FF6B3D?style=for-the-badge)](https://github.com/dhruvtoprani/Moo-Coin)

Moo-Coin is a prototype web-based interface for a social experiment around internet-native value, attention, and community behavior.

It turns a playful behavioral hypothesis into a concrete product surface: something people can see, react to, question, and interact with. The value of the project is the PM instinct behind it — taking an abstract social idea and making it testable through a lightweight interface.

## Product Signal

Moo-Coin sits at the intersection of social behavior, internet culture, and fast prototyping. It shows curiosity about how people respond to digital objects when they are wrapped in familiar product language: currency, value, attention, ownership, and community.

## What I Explored

- How naming and framing affect user perception
- How playful mechanics can make abstract social concepts easier to understand
- How a simple web interface can test behavior before a large build exists
- How to scope curiosity-driven ideas into small, concrete prototypes

## Why It Matters

Good product work often starts with a sharp question: *what would people do if this existed?* Moo-Coin reflects that habit of turning a social question into an interactive product experiment.
